# Annual Review (first two weeks of January)

The system-level honesty exercise. Question on the table every year:
**should this system continue to exist?** Passive indexing is the default
alternative and the burden of proof sits on the active system.

## 1. The honest math
- Full-year TWR (tools/metrics.py, flow-adjusted) vs QQQ buy-and-hold on
  the same capital, both **after costs and after tax**
  (knowledge/tax-basics §5: ST/LT drag computed from realized gains, not
  estimated).
- Risk-adjusted: Sharpe/Sortino/MaxDD vs benchmark; time underwater.
- Three-line verdict: beat / matched / lost to the passive alternative,
  after everything. No adjectives.

## 2. Continue / modify / terminate
Apply PERFORMANCE_EVALUATION self-termination gates on the full year.
Recommendation with evidence: continue as-is · continue modified (specific
Art V proposals) · revert to paper · index. If the strategy's edge is
smaller than its tax-and-cost drag, say "index" plainly.

## 3. Decision-quality year in review
- Calibration curve for the year (stated vs realized, calibration_log.csv).
- The 3 best and 3 worst decision *processes* (not outcomes) with lessons.
- Behavioral audit: which FAILURE_MODES / behavioral-finance biases
  actually fired this year, and did the countermeasure work?
- Missed opportunities honestly reviewed: things the rules kept us out of —
  was the rule right anyway? (Usually yes; prove it or propose the change.)

## 4. Portfolio & strategy structure
- Year-end PORTFOLIO_REVIEW (full template).
- Playbook expectancy table for the year: any playbook with negative
  expectancy over ≥20 trades → suspension proposal.
- Watchlist turnover and hit rate: did watchlist names outperform
  non-watchlist candidates we passed on?

## 5. Knowledge & process maintenance
- knowledge/ index pass (README §maintenance): prune, fill gaps surfaced by
  the year's lessons, update reading-list with what was actually read.
- Template refinements from a year of use → research/README changelog note.
- Verify every standing document still describes actual practice; fix
  drift (doc bugs) on the spot; rule changes go through Art V.
- Tax year-end: 1099-B reconciliation vs journal; wash-sale incidents
  count as process violations if unplanned.

## 6. Next year
- Capital/scaling decision vs IMPLEMENTATION_ROADMAP tiers.
- The single biggest system weakness and the year's one structural
  improvement priority (one, not five).
- Write the annual letter: what we believed, what happened, what we
  learned, what changes — in plain language a stranger could audit.

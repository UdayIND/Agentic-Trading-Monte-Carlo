# ECONOMIC DATA — Releases, Reading, and Market Transmission

Reference module. Macro data is *context and risk posture* for this system
(no-entry windows, regime awareness) — never a standalone trade signal
(RESEARCH_PROCESS §3). Always primary sources: BLS, BEA, Census, Fed, ISM.

## 1. The release map

| Release | Source, cadence | What it measures | Market sensitivity |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPI / Core CPI | BLS, monthly | Consumer inflation; core ex food/energy | Very high — repricing of Fed path |
| PCE / Core PCE | BEA, monthly | Fed's *preferred* inflation gauge | High |
| PPI | BLS, monthly | Pipeline (producer) inflation | Medium; leads CPI goods |
| Nonfarm payrolls (NFP) | BLS, 1st Friday | Jobs added, unemployment rate, wage growth (AHE) | Very high |
| JOLTS | BLS, monthly (lagged) | Openings, quits — labor tightness | Medium |
| Weekly jobless claims | DOL, Thursdays | Highest-frequency labor read | Medium; trend > level |
| GDP | BEA, quarterly (3 vintages) | Growth; watch final sales & GDI cross-check | Medium (backward-looking) |
| ISM Manufacturing / Services PMI | ISM, monthly | Diffusion; >50 expansion. New orders = the leading component | High |
| Retail sales | Census, monthly | Consumer spend; "control group" feeds GDP | High |
| Consumer confidence / sentiment | Conference Board / UMich | Expectations; UMich inflation expectations matter to the Fed | Medium |
| Housing: starts, permits, NAHB, existing sales | Census/NAR, monthly | Most rate-sensitive sector; permits lead | Medium |
| FOMC decision + SEP/dots + presser | Fed, 8×/year | Policy rate, QT pace, projections | Very high |

Trading rule already in force: scheduled major releases create NO-ENTRY
windows (intelligence report flags them); we don't hold event risk we
weren't paid to hold.

## 2. Interest rates & the yield curve

- Fed funds sets the short end; the long end prices growth + inflation
  expectations + term premium.
- **Inversion** (2s10s, 3m10y negative) has preceded every US recession
  since the 1960s with long, variable lags — and the *re-steepening* is
  historically the nearer recession signal than the inversion itself.
- Real yields (TIPS) drive long-duration equity valuations; rising real
  yields compress high-multiple growth hardest.
- Rate *expectations* (fed funds futures / implied path) move markets;
  the actual decision usually only matters vs that path.

## 3. Credit spreads

IG and HY spreads over Treasuries = the bond market's default/stress price.
Equities can rally while spreads widen — historically, believe credit.
Widening HY spreads + narrowing equity breadth = classic late-cycle pair.
Levels: HY spreads <300bp complacent, >500bp stressed, >800bp crisis
(rough, era-dependent).

## 4. Inflation mechanics

Headline vs core; goods vs services; shelter's outsized, lagged CPI weight
(rent measures trail spot rents ~a year); wage-price feedback via services.
Base effects can make YoY optics improve while MoM run-rate stays hot —
compute the 3m annualized rate to see the present.

## 5. Currencies & commodities

- **DXY strength** tightens global conditions, pressures US multinational
  earnings (translation), squeezes EM dollar debtors.
- **Oil**: supply/demand + OPEC discipline; both an inflation input and a
  demand signal; energy capex cycles are long.
- **Gold**: real-rate inverse + reserve/CB demand + crisis hedge.
- **Copper**: industrial demand proxy ("Dr. Copper"), China-heavy.

## 6. Central banks beyond the Fed

ECB, BoJ, PBoC set the *global* liquidity tide. BoJ policy (yield-curve
control era, carry-trade unwind risk) can transmit violently to US assets
(Aug 2024 demonstration). Global liquidity matters to QQQ-type assets more
than most single US releases.

## 7. Reading discipline

1. **Revisions matter as much as prints** (esp. payrolls, retail sales).
2. **Trend > single print**; 3-month moving averages over monthly noise.
3. **Second-derivative thinking**: markets price acceleration/deceleration,
   not levels.
4. The reaction *to* the data is information: hawkish print + market rally
   = positioning was already there. Never fight the observed reaction with
   a narrative about what "should" have happened.
5. Every macro claim in an artifact carries source + date like any other
   claim; the weekly macro memo (RESEARCH_PROCESS §2.4) is the cache.

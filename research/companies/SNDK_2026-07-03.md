## 1. Business model

SanDisk manufactures NAND flash memory and SSDs (solid-state drives). ~70% of revenue is NAND flash chips (sold to OEMs, data centers, cloud); ~30% is branded consumer/enterprise storage products (USB drives, SD cards, portable SSDs). Historically a consumer-focused company; now pivoting hard to data-center NAND (higher margins). Business model is fab-light (outsources manufacturing).

## 2. Industry & competitive position

NAND flash is a duopoly: Samsung (~37% share), Kioxia (~20%), Micron (~15%), Western Digital (~12%), SK Hynix (~10%), others. SanDisk competes as an **OEM supplier** (not a retailer). Market drivers: AI data-center buildout is driving 645% YoY surge in SanDisk's datacenter segment ($1.47B in Q3 vs $215M YoY). Competitive positioning: SanDisk is the #2 NAND supplier (after Samsung) by volume and the most aggressively expanding capacity to capture AI demand.

## 3. Moat

**Claimed:** Technology leadership in NAND (process node parity with Samsung); datacenter customer relationships (hyperscaler OEMs prefer US + Taiwan-aligned suppliers over South Korea); supply-chain control (NAND production bottleneck = pricing power).

**Evidence FOR:**
- Q3 2026: Datacenter segment +645% YoY ($1.47B), with NAND prices up 70–75% sequentially [web: market reports].
- Revenue $5.95B +251% YoY; EPS $23.41 beat 60% vs consensus.
- Q4 guidance: $7.75–8.25B revenue (implies $2.1B+ datacenter); EPS $30–33 (+26% from Q3).
- NAND supply is a bottleneck (Samsung capacity-constrained for AI, Kioxia/SK Hynix similarly limited); SanDisk is the marginal supplier getting price-making power.
- **High Bandwidth Flash (HBF):** R&D project (first samples H2 2026, commercial 2027) could double TAM if AI vendors adopt. This is the multi-year upside.

**Evidence AGAINST / erosion vectors:**
- NAND is an extremely cyclical commodity (like DRAM). Once supply opens, prices collapse 50%+.
- Samsung is the technology leader and can undercut SanDisk on price if it chooses (competitive moat is WEAK).
- HBF is still in samples; adoption is SPECULATIVE (not a guarantee).
- Geopolitical: SanDisk is US-Japan-owned but manufactures via Kioxia (Japan-Korea JV); US-China export bans could restrict supply (both negative for revenue).
- At +858% H1 rally, valuation is stretched; profit-taking (−11% Jul 2) is normal.

**Verdict:** Moat is NARROW (supply advantage lasts 12–18 months max before capacity normalizes). HBF is a long-term optionality, not a current revenue driver. **This is more speculative than Micron** (Micron has SCAs; SNDK relies on spot-market pricing power). Upsdie is higher; downside is steeper.

## 7. Bull case (steelman)

SanDisk is the #2 NAND supplier and the biggest beneficiary of the AI data-center buildout. Datacenter is now 24% of revenue (growing at +600% YoY); this segment can reach $10B+/year if the AI capex cycle sustains. NAND prices are up 70–75% and should hold elevated through 2027 as supply remains tight. HBF (high-bandwidth flash) is a multi-year project that could double TAM if hyperscalers adopt it for model storage (complementary to HBM DRAM). Earnings can sustain $70–100/share through 2027. Stock at $1743 on 18–20× multiple is justified if growth sustains. Upside to $2500–3000 if HBF adoption accelerates.

## 8. Bear case (steelman)

NAND prices are commodity-cyclical and are already elevated from peak demand. Once Samsung, Kioxia, and SK Hynix ramp capacity (2027–2028), NAND ASP (average selling price) collapses 50–60% (historical precedent: 2018–2019). Margins compress from 75% → 40% within 12–18 months. Unlike Micron, SanDisk has NO long-term contracts (spot-market dependent); when prices fall, revenue evaporates fast. EPS collapses from $100 to $15–20/share. Multiple de-rates from 20× → 12× = −70% total decline (to $180–240). HBF is 12–18 months away from samples and adoption timeline is SPECULATIVE; don't bet on it. Stock is in peak-earnings mania; profit-taking (-14% today) is just the start. Downside to $300–400 is plausible within 18 months.

## 9. Risks (ranked by probability × severity)

1. **Spot NAND price collapse (35% × high):** Supply opens 2027–2028; datacenter customers renegotiate; ASP falls 50%; revenue + margin compression = −60% earnings, −70% stock price.
2. **Multiple compression (30% × high):** Even if earnings hold, valuation from 20× → 12× = −40% on multiple alone.
3. **HBF delays / adoption risk (20% × medium):** If HBF samples slip to 2027 or adoption rates are low, growth driver disappears; disappointment cascade.
4. **Geopolitical export restrictions (15% × medium):** If US bans NAND sales to China (SanDisk China revenue ~$500M), earnings down 10%.
5. **Capex intensity surprise (10% × low):** If NAND ramp requires 20%+ capex, FCF turns negative; cash burn.
6. **Macro recession (10% × high):** Capex cycles pause; datacenter buildout stops; revenue halves; earnings collapse.

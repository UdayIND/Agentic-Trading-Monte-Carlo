## 1. Business model

Micron manufactures DRAM (dynamic RAM) and NAND flash memory chips. DRAM is the primary revenue driver (~55–60% historically) used in every computing device; NAND is secondary (~40–45%) used in SSDs, storage. Micron operates as a fab-light company (outsources manufacturing to foundries). Revenue is cyclical but currently in a structural upswing driven by AI infrastructure buildout.

## 2. Industry & competitive position

Memory is a duopoly/oligopoly: Micron, Samsung, SK Hynix + Kioxia (NAND-only). Micron is the only major US/Allied memory producer (strategic importance). Market share: Micron ~21% DRAM (vs Samsung ~45%, SK Hynix ~30%), ~15% NAND (Samsung ~37%, Kioxia ~20%). Competitive positioning: Micron historically competed on cost; now competing on speed/performance (HBM = high-bandwidth memory for AI accelerators; NAND pricing power from supply constraints).

## 3. Moat

**Claimed:** Technology leadership (HBM process node parity with Samsung); supply-chain control (multi-year SCAs with hyperscalers); cost advantage (fab utilization at capacity).

**Evidence FOR:**
- Q3 2026: 16 Strategic Customer Agreements signed (multi-year, take-or-pay), covering ~20% DRAM and ~33% NAND volume. These are *binding contracts* with price bands, not spot-market dependent.
- 84.6% gross margin Q3 (vs 37.7% YoY) = massive pricing power and/or operational leverage.
- Operating cash flow $45.7B in quarter (annualized ~$180B) — strongest in company history.
- Debt cut by $4.4B in Q3 alone; net cash position $30.2B− $5.7B = ~$24.5B net cash (fortress balance sheet).

**Evidence AGAINST / erosion vectors:**
- Supply will eventually normalize: once more fabs come online (Samsung, Kioxia new capacity 2027–2028), NAND pricing collapses back to cost.
- Samsung is more efficient (lower cost structure); if Samsung ramps HBM, Micron loses pricing power.
- Geopolitical: China restrictions on advanced chips; potential ban on US supply could force China into domestic memory (risk to Micron's long-term volume).
- Multiple compression: even if earnings hold, a 30× forward P/E → 20× multiple compression = −33% price, independent of earnings.

**Verdict:** Moat is NARROW for the next 12–18 months (SCAs protect volume + pricing). Erodes significantly 2027+ when supply normalizes. **This is a cyclical peak, not a permanent franchise.** We're betting on the SCA protection lasting through 2027.

## 7. Bull case (steelman)

Micron is the only US memory producer. SCAs provide contractual protection through 2027+ at high margins. HBM technology leadership is real (parity with Samsung, ahead of SK Hynix in speed). AI data-center buildout will require $500B+ capex over 2027–2029; Micron captured supply contracts locking in 20–33% of this volume. Earnings can sustain $80+/share through 2027, justifying 12–15× multiple = $960–1200/share. Operating leverage at scale is extraordinary; FCF generation can sustain buybacks + dividends (positive for shareholders). **Geopolitical:** US government may subsidize memory production (Taiwan/Korea supply risk); Micron is the strategic hedge.

## 8. Bear case (steelman)

Memory is the ultimate cyclical commodity. When supply opens (2027–2028), NAND prices collapse 50%+ (historical pattern: 2018–2019). Gross margins compress from 84% → 40–50% within 12–18 months (mean reversion). SCAs protect volume but price bands probably include downside triggers; if market crashes, Micron renegotiates downward. Multiple compression happens faster than earnings (multiple goes 15× → 10× before earnings even decline); −40% total decline is plausible. **Competition:** Samsung has lower cost structure; if Samsung innovates HBM faster, Micron's technology moat evaporates. Stock is priced for perfection; any stumble triggers selloff.

6. **Macro recession (10% × high):** If US enters recession, capex cycles pause; memory demand collapses; earnings down 70%.
